UC San Diego: April 2018
Instructional Assistant



After two quarters at UC San Diego I was offered an IA (instructional assistant) position for the third-ever offering of a new data science class, DSC 10: Principles of Data Science. I am excited to be part of a brand new major, and to be among the people who can open new students' eyes to the world of data science.

This quarter, the CSE Department didn't want the Data Science program taking all of the graduate student TA's, so I had to assume many of the roles of a TA such as: creating, revising, and grading homework and project assignments; grading exams, creating exam solutions, and proctoring exams; and participating heavily in course organization and management. I did this all in addition to holding office hours to tutor students. I wrote about some more exciting experiences here.

Chapman University: April 2018
ASA DataFest



With a team of four other students in my major, we represented UC San Diego at the ASA (American Statistical Association) DataFest held at Chapman University. We had a midterm right before the event started — and the drive took a few hours — so after getting a late start we were in an exceptionally crunchy time crunch to fulfill what we were tasked with upon arrival.

We were given an anonymized dataset from indeed.com, and tasked to come up with an interesting question and create a short business pitch to present to indeed by the end of the hackathon. After plenty of stress and sleep depravation, we posed a question to find out what cities have the best prospects for different types of jobs and we created an algorithm to rank and recommend cities that are well suited for a given job industry.
